Know Before You Go

Difficulty Guide

Understanding trek difficulty helps you choose the right adventure. Here's what each level means for you.

Easy

Suitable for first-timers and families. Short daily distances, minimal elevation gain. Basic fitness is enough.

5–10 km per day
Max altitude: 3,500m
No prior experience needed
Ideal for: Families, Beginners

Moderate

Requires reasonable fitness and some trekking experience. Involves longer trails and higher elevations.

10–18 km per day
Altitude: 3,500–4,500m
4–6 weeks preparation
Ideal for: Active adults

Hard / Expedition

For seasoned trekkers only. High altitudes, demanding ascents, technical terrain and glacier crossings.

18–25 km per day
Altitude: 4,500m+
2–3 months preparation
Ideal for: Experienced trekkers

Trek FAQs

Everything you need to know before you hit the trail.

Essentials include layered clothing (thermal base + fleece + windproof jacket), waterproof trekking boots, trekking poles, sunscreen SPF 50+, headlamp, personal first-aid, and a 40–50L backpack. We provide a detailed packing list after booking.

Start 4–8 weeks before: daily walking/running (5km+), stair climbing, cycling, and light gym. For Hard treks, include long hikes with a loaded backpack. We send a personalised fitness plan with every booking.

All our guides are altitude medicine trained. Itineraries include proper acclimatisation rest days. We carry supplemental oxygen and Diamox tablets. Descent is always the best cure and we never hesitate to prioritise safety.

Absolutely. Many of our trekkers are solo women and we pride ourselves on creating a safe, respectful environment. We also have women guides available on request. Safety is our top priority, always.

Easy treks: 10+ years (with parents). Moderate treks: 16+ years. Hard/Expedition treks: 18+ years. Children under 16 require parent/guardian consent and special consideration. We assess on a case-by-case basis.

Safety is paramount. If weather makes a trek unsafe, we offer a full reschedule at no extra cost OR a 90% refund. We monitor conditions 48 hours before departure and communicate promptly.
